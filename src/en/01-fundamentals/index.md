# Module 1: Fundamentals and Roots

Like with almost everything, the best way to start is at the beginning. Before talking about frameworks, architecture, artificial intelligence, cloud or design patterns, we need to go back to the roots...
**what is software really?**, where does it come from, how did we get here and why did this discipline end up becoming one of the foundations of the modern world.

This module starts with a simple idea: **to make better decisions going forward, we first need to understand where we come from**. Many solutions that seem new today were already thought of before, with different names, different machines and different constraints. Knowing that history is not technical nostalgia. It is judgment. It helps us recognize patterns, avoid repeated mistakes and understand that every abstraction we use exists because someone, at some point, had to solve a real problem.

Here we will look at software from its fundamentals: the inseparable relationship between hardware and code, the birth of computing as a discipline, the evolution towards software engineering and the importance of remembering that every program, no matter how elegant it looks, ends up running on a machine. A mobile app, a web page, a car screen, a smart TV or a restaurant system are not magic. They are layers of abstraction over real hardware. And the better we understand those layers, the less we will be debugging in the air and the closer we will be to thinking like true engineers.
